How it's worked out

  • Source: 3 complete years of Ballarat REX sales (2023–25).
  • Each historical sale's SKU is mapped to its current product type (so a REX taxonomy change doesn't skew the curve).
  • Within-year normalisation: each month's share of its own year is taken, then averaged across years and ×12 — this strips year-over-year growth so the number is pure seasonality, not "was that a big year".
  • So index = average month's share × 12. 1.00 = average; 1.50 = sells 50% above its yearly average that month.
  • Confidence reflects sample size; thin categories (LOW) fall back to the store-wide baseline so we never trust a noisy curve.

How it factors into Smart Ordering

The Smart model scales the current sell-rate by how the order window compares to now:

seasonal_factor = avg(index over [today … today+lead+horizon])
                  ÷ index[current month]
adj_daily       = current_velocity × seasonal_factor
target_stock    = adj_daily × (lead + horizon)
  • Ramping into a busy season → factor >1, pre-stocks ahead of the climb.
  • Heading into a quiet season → factor <1, trims before the drop.
  • Mid-plateau (already in season and staying) → factor ≈1, since the live velocity already reflects the season — no double-counting.

The Seas× column on the Smart Order tab is exactly this factor per SKU.

{# 1. INPUTS #}

1 · Inputs

• rex_ballarat_inventory — available, MSL, on-order, lead time, supplier, product type
• ballaratv2 sales — 7/14/30/90/365-day units, same-window 1&2yr-ago, active sale-days, biggest single order, days-since-last-sale
• neto_stock_situation_unkitted — the active+approved "allowed to order" list
• rex_seasonality_index — per-category monthly seasonal curve
• rex_po_back_order — SKUs already on open back-order
• po_bucket_mapping — which of the 3 POs each product type goes to
{# 2. GATES #}

2 · Eligibility gates — a SKU must pass ALL six or it never appears

① supplier = JONO AND JOHNO
② available ≥ 0 (negatives → reconcile, not order)
③ SKU is in the active+approved NETO list
④ not disabled in REX
⑤ not on back-order
⑥ product type has a bucket (1/2/3)
{# 3. SIGNALS #}

3 · Signals (computed per SKU)

Velocity (outlier-resistant)
if one order in 180d > 0.5 × the 30-day total AND 30d < 90d ÷ 3:
    daily = sold_90d / 90      ← a one-off bulk sale is discounted
else:
    daily = sold_30d / 30
Seasonal factor (span-weighted)
seasonal_factor = avg(index over [today … today+lead+horizon]) ÷ index[this month]
adj_daily       = velocity × seasonal_factor

Category curve when its confidence is HIGH/MED, else the store baseline. >1 ramps up before a busy season, <1 trims before a quiet one.

Position & coverage
net_position  = available + on_order
coverage_days = available / velocity         ← days to stockout at today's rate
target_stock  = ceil(adj_daily × (lead + 30))   ← cover lead time + 30-day horizon
{# 4. DECISION #}

4 · Decision (first match wins)

{% for cond, cat, desc, col in [ ('days since last sale > 90', 'DEAD', 'no recent demand → skip', 'text-slate-500'), ('≤ 2 active sale-days in 90d (but did sell)', 'SPIKY', 'lumpy → top up to its biggest-ever single order', 'text-indigo-600'), ('target_stock − net_position ≤ 0', 'COVERED', 'already have enough → skip', 'text-emerald-600'), ('coverage_days < lead time', 'URGENT', 'will stock out before a PO can land → order now', 'text-red-600'), ('otherwise', 'ORDER', 'order the shortfall to target', 'text-blue-600'), ] %}
{{ cond|safe }} {{ cat }} {{ desc }}
{% endfor %}
{# 5. QUANTITY #}

5 · Quantity

DEAD / COVERED   → 0
SPIKY            → max(0, biggest_single_order − net_position)
URGENT / ORDER   → max(1, target_stock − net_position)

then carton-round → nearest multiple of REX carton qty, min 1 carton
{# 6. URGENCY #}

6 · Urgency label (triage)

CRITICAL · coverage < lead HIGH · coverage < lead + 7d MEDIUM · coverage < lead + 30d LOW · comfortable
