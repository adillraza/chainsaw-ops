How it's worked out

  • Source: 3 complete years of Ballarat REX sales (2023–25).
  • Each historical sale's SKU is mapped to its current product type (so a REX taxonomy change doesn't skew the curve).
  • Within-year normalisation: each month's share of its own year is taken, then averaged across years and ×12 — this strips year-over-year growth so the number is pure seasonality, not "was that a big year".
  • So index = average month's share × 12. 1.00 = average; 1.50 = sells 50% above its yearly average that month.
  • Confidence reflects sample size; thin categories (LOW) fall back to the store-wide baseline so we never trust a noisy curve.

How it factors into Smart Ordering

The Smart model scales the current sell-rate by how the order window compares to now:

seasonal_factor = avg(index over [today … today+lead+horizon])
                  ÷ index[current month]
adj_daily       = current_velocity × seasonal_factor
target_stock    = adj_daily × (lead + horizon)
  • Ramping into a busy season → factor >1, pre-stocks ahead of the climb.
  • Heading into a quiet season → factor <1, trims before the drop.
  • Mid-plateau (already in season and staying) → factor ≈1, since the live velocity already reflects the season — no double-counting.

The Seas× column on the Smart Order tab is exactly this factor per SKU.
